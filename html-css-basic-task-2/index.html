Vietnamese Traditional Craft Villages

Introduction

Vietnam is a one-party Communist state and has one of south-east Asia's fastest-growing economies and has set its sights on becoming a developed nation by 2020.
The country has a population of around 92 millions people. Major language is Vietnamese and English is really popular here. The capital of Vietnam is Hanoi, in which contains many different Traditional Craft Villages.

Traditional Craft Villages are spread all over the country. In this website, you will see only the most popular ones and they are well-known not only in Vietnam but worldwide. Vietnam has approximately 1500 handicraft villages. These villages are the soul of the whole country since they keep the beauty of the cultural part of Vietnam.

Bat Trang Ceramics Village

 Learn More

Bat Trang Ceramics Village is one of the most famous Handicraft Village in Vietnam. The village is located in Hanoi, Vietnam's Capital. They are famous for beautiful porcelain artworks which are created by a combination of traditional and modern techniques. They produce the finest handmade ceramics products in Vietnam.
You could see the artisans create the product right in front of your eyes.

Dong Ho Folk Painting Village

 Learn More

Dong Ho village is located in Song Ho Commune, Thuan Thanh District, Bac Ninh Province. Dong Ho paintings have about 300 years of history in the north of Vietnam.
Dong Ho pictures are printed on a special kind of Dzo paper. The printing paper is made of bark of a tree called “Dzo”.

Dong Ho paintings used to be produced before Tet - Vietnamese New Year's Eve. Therefore, they are many illustrations of Tet in Dong Ho paintings.

Ha Dong Silk Village

 Learn More

Ha Dong Silk or Van Phuc Silk Village is situated on the banks of Nhue Thi River, Nguyen Trai Road, near Ha Dong city, 8 km southwest of Hanoi. The village has been much well known for its traditional sericulture, weaving, and silk products. Tourists coming here are lured by various beautiful shirts, crafts, ties, dresses and many other things made of silk available in the village.
Ha Dong Silk Village is the cradle of Vietnamese Silk.

Tay Ho Conical Hat Village

The village is located in Phu Ho commune, Phu Vang district, Thua Thien-Hue province, near Nhu Y river. Conical hat - "Non La" and Vietnamese Traditional Dress - "Ao Dai" are considered as symbols of Vietnam, especially Thua Thien Hue - a Vietnamese province.
